Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

Columns included in this dataset:

  • PatientId: Identification of the patient
  • AppointmentID: Identification of the Appointment
  • Gender: Gender Type of patient (M/F)
  • ScheduledDay: Datetime of appointment registration
  • AppointmentDay: Datetime of the actual appointment
  • Age: Age of the patient
  • Neighbourhood: Patient's neighbourhood
  • Scholarship: Signify whether the patient is sponsored by the government (True/False)
  • Hipertension: Indicate whether the patient suffers from Hipertension (True/False)
  • Diabetes: Indicate whether the patient suffers from Diabetes (True/False)
  • Alcoholism: Indicate whether the patient deals with alcoholism (True/False)
  • Handcap: Indicate whether the patient suffers from a disability (True/False)
  • SMS_received: Indicate whether the patient has recieved reminder SMS (True/False)
  • No-show: Indicate whether the patient DID NOT attend the appointment (True/False)

'''
This function was created to simplify and increase the efficency of a great portion of the code in this section
it takes in a column -or a list of columns- to check against the "No-show" column to get the mean of skipping the appointment, 
it will then plot the results and return the output for the groupby method.
'''
def mean_and_plot(col):
    df.groupby(col)['No-show'].mean().plot(y="Skipping Mean",x=col,title='{} Vs Attendance'.format(col),kind='bar')
    plt.title('{} vs Skipping'.format(col))
    plt.ylabel('Skipping Mean')
    plt.xlabel(col)
    return df.groupby(col)['No-show'].mean()

'''
This function takes in a column as parameter and calculates the percentage of attendance for that specific column (i.e chronic patients , alcoholic patients, ...etc), 
plots a bar graph of attendance vs skipped and return the percentage of attendance
'''
def pct_att(var):
    filtered_df=df[var]==True
    total_num = filtered_df.sum()
    num_attended = total_num - df['No-show'][filtered_df].sum()
    num_skipped = df['No-show'][filtered_df].sum()
    pct_attended = num_attended * 100 / total_num 
    x = ['Attended','Skipped']
    y = [num_attended,num_skipped]
    plt.title("{} Patients Attendance".format(var))
    plt.ylabel('Number of patients')
    plt.xlabel('Attendance')
    plt.bar(x,y)
    return pct_attended

Conclusions¶

Analysing this data has yielded some interesting findings, I'll present the findings as answers to the previously stated questions in the introduction

  1. What is the mean age of the patients who show up vs the patients who doesn't?
    • The mean age of the patients who show up is 37.8 years old, while the mean age of the patients who skip their appointment is 34.32 years old
  2. Does gender play a role in showing up for the appointment?
    • Based on the data, it seems that gender -on its own- plays little to no role in indicating whether the patient will attend the appointment
  3. Are sponsored patients more likely to show up for the appointment?
    • Taking the sponsorship data on its own, the data indicates that there is a higher chance for a sponsored patient to SKIP their appointment
  4. What is the pct of patients with chronic illness (Hipertension and/or diabetes) that shows up for appt ?
    • Chronic patients are more likely to attend their appointment, around 82.54% of chronic patients do
  5. What is the pct of alcoholic patients show up for the appt?
    • Alcoholism doesn't clearly indicate whether it'll affect whether a patient will attend or skip the appointment, around 80% of alcoholic patients attend their appointment
  6. What is the pct of alcoholic patients show up for the appt (Gender Based)?
    • When adding gender to the equation, the data suggests that alcoholic females are more likely to SKIP their appointment compared to alcoholic males and both non-alcoholic males and females
  7. Does recieving an SMS increase the chance of showing up for the appt ?
    • According to the Data, those who recieved an sms were more likely to skip their appointment, however there are simply not enough data to prove this, as corelation doesn't imply causation (Data Limitation)
  8. What is the highest and lowest neighbourhood in term of attendence?
    • The highest neighbourhood in term of attendance is PARQUE INDUSTRIAL
    • The lowest neighbourhood in term of attendance is ILHAS OCENICAS DE TRINDADE
  9. Out of all the neighbourhoods, which gender have the highest/lowest attendance?
    • when bringing gender into the equation, ILHAS OCENICAS DE TRINDADE remains the lowest in term of attendance with females as the lowest attendees
    • On the other hand, the highest attendance changes to Males in AEROPORTO
  10. Is same day appointment met more frequently?
    • Same-Day appointment are met Significantly more frequently which was a very interesting find
  11. Do sponsored handicapped patients make their appointment more frequently?
    • Even though in an earlier question, there was an indication that sponsored patients are more likely to skip their appointment, upon checking the data against handicapped patients as well, I found that sponsored handicapped patients are the least likely to skip their appointment out of that specific group
